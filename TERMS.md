# Terms of Use

The minimal rules for using CanadaScanada. Plain language, no lawyer-shaped
prose where prose works.

## Using the site

You can read this site, share its content, link to it, cite it in your
own work, fork it for your own purposes (see `FORKING.md`), and feed
its data into your own analysis tools.

You may not:

- Frame the site or its content as your own work or your own
  publication. The data licence (CC-BY-4.0) requires attribution, and
  editorial content is all-rights-reserved.
- Use this site to harass, threaten, or doxx any person named in the
  events. The corpus exists to support informed civic participation,
  not as ammunition for harassment.
- Republish editorial content (memos, original prose, branding)
  without permission. See `LICENSE-editorial.md`.
- Scrape the site for AI training purposes without explicit permission.
  The data licence permits reuse with attribution; AI training is a
  use case that requires its own conversation.
- Attempt to compromise the site's infrastructure (the GitHub repo, the
  build pipeline, the hosting provider). Static-HTML architecture has
  a small attack surface, but normal cybercrime laws apply if you try
  anyway.

## Submitting feedback

When you submit feedback via the form at `canadascanada.ca/feedback`,
you affirm:

- The information you provide is accurate to the best of your
  knowledge.
- You have the right to share any source URLs you cite.
- You are not submitting material that violates someone else's
  privacy, copyright, or other rights.

You retain ownership of the words you submit. By submitting, you
grant the project a non-exclusive licence to:

- Review your submission
- Quote brief excerpts in `CORRECTIONS.md` if your submission leads to
  a correction
- Credit you (with permission) or note "submitted by a reader" in any
  resulting correction entry

The project does not claim ownership over your submitted facts or
sources. Facts are not owned. Sources have their own copyright held by
their original publishers.
